Maya 3D

Textures

These are the first sculptures that I have made in Maya. I created a ballon animal giraffe and experimented with the different preset textures by assigning new materials and applying a balloon, jade, and velvet texture. I also applied two lights from the front and back that illuminate the object.


Body Scan

Our class got to use a 3D scanner to scan our bodies in a position of our choosing. We then exported the file into Maya and were told to attach a prosthetic extension of our own creation. I stood up with my arms out and envisioned sculpting wings and attaching them under my arms. This project helped me become familiar with Maya’s sculpting tools. I then added a waxy texture to the finished product.


Virtual Installation

This is another project in which we were told to create 3 objects in a virtual installation. I started with creating a glass with a glass texture applied as well as skin texture for the olives and wood texture on the toothpick. The lighting creates a classy and seductive setting.

I added to the scenery by creating a table with a marble texture applied through an image. The background is a city scene which shows through the reflection on the glass.


Bouncing Ball Animation

This is the first simple animation of a bounding ball that I have made in Maya. I used the graph editor to make the bouncing more realistc. Click the image to play the animation.


WIP: Bee Animation

This is the most recent project I am working on in Maya. These sculpted bees are going to be the subject in a short animation that I am creating.

For this video I am setting the scene of a peaceful grassy hilly meadow in which the bees are flying toward some flourishing pink flowers. While they are on their way over, the hills catch on fire and red light floods the landscape and before they can reach the flowers, they start to wilt and are unable to pollinate them.

I want this piece to be a reminder of the poor state that our earth is STILL in and how it is increasingly getting worse. I chose the bees since they were at one point a universal symbol of how bad global warming was and how the poor conditions caused them to disappear at rapid rates.
